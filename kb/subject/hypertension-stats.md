# Hypertension: Global Statistics & Guidelines

*Last updated: 2026-04-07*

---

## Prevalence

### Global
- **1.28 billion adults** aged 30-79 worldwide have hypertension (WHO, 2023)
- **~46% are unaware** they have it
- Only **~21%** have it under control
- Hypertension is the **#1 modifiable risk factor** for cardiovascular disease and premature death
- Sources:
  - WHO. "Hypertension Key Facts." September 2023.
  - NCD Risk Factor Collaboration (NCD-RisC). "Worldwide trends in hypertension prevalence and progress in treatment and control from 1990 to 2019." *The Lancet*, 2021; 398(10304), 957-980. DOI: 10.1016/S0140-6736(21)01330-1. (Pooled data from 1,201 studies, 104 million individuals.)

### United States
- **~119.9 million adults** (47% of adult population) have hypertension
- Only **~24%** have it controlled (<130/80)
- Higher in Black adults (~57%), men (~50% vs ~44% women), adults over 65 (~74%)
- Costs US healthcare ~$131 billion/year
- Source: CDC NCHS / NHANES data; AHA Heart Disease and Stroke Statistics 2024 Update.

### United Kingdom
- **~14.4 million adults** in England have high blood pressure (~1 in 3 adults)
- Only **~58%** diagnosed; of those, only **~29%** controlled
- Contributes to **~75,000 deaths/year** in the UK
- Source: Health Survey for England; British Heart Foundation CVD Statistics Compendium 2023-2024.

## BP Classification

### AHA/ACC 2017 (Used in US, in our app)

| Category | Systolic (mmHg) | Diastolic (mmHg) |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Normal | <120 | AND <80 |
| Elevated | 120-129 | AND <80 |
| High Stage 1 | 130-139 | OR 80-89 |
| High Stage 2 | >=140 | OR >=90 |
| Hypertensive Crisis | >180 | AND/OR >120 |

Source: Whelton, P.K. et al. (2018). "2017 ACC/AHA Guideline for Prevention, Detection, Evaluation, and Management of High Blood Pressure in Adults." *JACC*, 71(19), e127-e248. DOI: 10.1016/j.jacc.2017.11.006

### NICE NG136 (Used in UK)

| Category | Clinic BP | Home/Ambulatory BP |
|----------|-----------|---------------------|
| Hypertension threshold | >=140/90 | >=135/85 |

**Note:** UK threshold is 10 mmHg higher than US. This means more people are classified as hypertensive in the US than UK for the same readings.

## Lifestyle Interventions Ranked by Evidence

| Intervention | Systolic Reduction | Source |
|-------------|-------------------|--------|
| DASH diet | ~11 mmHg | AHA 2017 guidelines |
| Weight loss (per kg) | ~1 mmHg per kg | Multiple meta-analyses |
| Sodium reduction (<1.5g/day) | ~5-6 mmHg | DASH-Sodium trial |
| **IMST (5 min/day)** | **~9 mmHg** | Craighead et al. 2021 |
| Physical activity (150 min/week) | ~5-8 mmHg | AHA 2017 guidelines |
| **Slow breathing (6/min, 15 min/day)** | **~4-15 mmHg** | Multiple RCTs |
| Alcohol moderation | ~4 mmHg | AHA 2017 guidelines |

Breathing exercises rank alongside exercise and sodium reduction in evidence strength. IMST is remarkable for requiring only 5 min/day.

## Regulatory Landscape

| Category | FDA Regulation |
|----------|---------------|
| General wellness apps (breathing, tracking) | **Not regulated** |
| Apps claiming to diagnose or treat | **Regulated** (e.g., RESPeRATE is FDA-cleared) |
| BP measurement devices | **FDA Class II** medical device |
| Phone camera "BP measurement" | **Not accurate/cleared** — apps claiming this face regulatory risk |

### Key distinction for our app
BP Helper is a **wellness app** (tracking + breathing exercises). It does NOT claim to diagnose, treat, or measure blood pressure. This keeps it outside FDA regulation. The disclaimer "not medical advice, consult your doctor" is essential.
