## Direct Competitors

### 1. BreathNow (Cardio Calm Ltd)
- **Developer:** Dmitri Konash (CS PhD, former hi-tech exec) / Cardio Calm Limited (UK), Tax ID: 14030124
- **Platforms:** iOS (4.7/5, ~2,748 ratings), Android (Google Play)
- **Pricing:** Free + subscriptions ($6.99 - $49.99 tiers)
- **Key features:** BP tracking, breathing exercises (4-7-8, box, IMST, slow BP), AI Health Coach "Flow", HRV tracking, stress monitor, VO2Max test (Cambridge research), Apple Watch integration
- **Differentiator:** Only app combining BP tracking AND reduction in one platform. "Closes the loop" between measurement and intervention
- **Content ecosystem:** Medium blog, YouTube, Udemy courses, Facebook group, TikTok
- **Recent moves:** Nutrition/meal analysis (Feb 2026), AI Coach (Oct 2025)
- **Revenue/Downloads:** No public estimates available from Sensor Tower/Appfigures/data.ai. App analytics platforms require paid accounts to access BreathNow-specific data. Given ~2,748 iOS ratings and niche positioning, estimated low single-digit thousands monthly downloads
- **Weaknesses:** English only, one-person dev team (scale risk), mixed Pro reviews
- **Sources:** [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/breathnow-blood-pressure-app/id1551799152) | [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.dkonash.breathnow) | [Website](https://www.breathnow.app/)

### 2. SmartBP (Evolve Medical Systems)
- **Developer:** Evolve Medical Systems, LLC
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android, Apple Watch
- **Pricing:** Freemium with multiple tiers:
  - Free (ad-supported, basic tracking)
  - Weekly: $0.99
  - Monthly: $2.99
  - Annual: $8.99-$12.99 (with/without trial)
  - Lifetime: $19.99-$29.99
  - Multi-user profile add-on: $5.99
- **App Store Rating:** iOS 4.51/5 (~50K ratings) | Android 3.54/5 (~5.6K ratings)
- **Key features:** Manual BP entry + auto-sync from Bluetooth monitors via Apple Health/Google Fit, immediate feedback against AHA/ESC guidelines, trend charts and insights, PDF/CSV export for doctors, Siri + Apple Intelligence voice logging, Apple Watch app
- **Differentiator:** Pure simplicity — does one thing (BP tracking) extremely well. Best-in-class report generation for sharing with healthcare providers. Siri integration is unique
- **Weaknesses:** No intervention/breathing component, no guided exercises, Android version significantly lower-rated
- **Sources:** [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/blood-pressure-tracker-smartbp/id519076558) | [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.smartbloodpressure) | [Website](https://www.smartbp.app/)

### 3. Qardio (Qardio Inc.) — DEFUNCT as of late 2025
- **Developer:** Qardio Inc., San Francisco. Founded 2012 by Marco Peluso & Rosario Iannella
- **Status:** BANKRUPT. Domain qardio.com sold Nov 2, 2025 on Namecheap. App removed from App Store. Trustpilot rating: 1.2/5
- **Hardware ecosystem (was):** QardioArm ($53-99), QardioArm 2, QardioBase (smart scale), QardioBase 2, QardioCore (ECG), QardioSpO2, QardioTemp
- **App pricing (was):** Free app + Qardio+ subscription ($9.99/month) for full features
- **Funding:** Reported between $27M-$104M across sources (discrepancies). CB Insights reported $67.18M over 9 rounds
- **Key features (were):** FDA-cleared wireless BP monitor, multi-device health ecosystem, QardioMD doctor dashboard, LIVE ECG remote monitoring
- **Differentiator (was):** Vertically integrated hardware+software, medical-grade devices
- **What happened:** CEO change in 2021 (Mike Alvarez from Medtronic), strategic pivot from consumer to professional health. Company went bankrupt; domain sold; community app "OfflineQArm" and "LibreArm" created by users to keep hardware alive
- **Lesson for new entrants:** Hardware lock-in is risky. When the company dies, users lose everything. Software-only + device-agnostic = more resilient
- **Sources:** [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qardio) | [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/qardio.com) | [Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/qardio) | [LibreArm](https://ptylr.com/posts/2025-09-28-librearm-breathing-new-life-into-qardioarm-devices)

### 4. Welltory (Welltory Inc.)
- **Developer:** Welltory Inc. (San Francisco)
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android
- **Pricing:** ~$12/month (Premium). Annual and Lifetime plans available. 3-day free trial with full access. Free tier retains basic self-tracking
- **App Store Rating:** 4.8/5 (iOS). Trustpilot: 3.5/5 (127 reviews)
- **Users:** 16M+ across 24 countries
- **Funding:** $12M total. Most recent: $2M credit line from Braavo Capital (2025) for AI health companion launch. Earlier: $1M raise in 2017
- **Revenue:** 70% YoY growth. Ranked #31 in Deloitte SF Bay Area Technology Fast 500
- **Key features:** HRV measurement via camera + wearables, stress/energy/burnout risk scores, sleep analysis, AI-powered personalised recommendations, workout recovery insights, 1,000+ data source integrations (Fitbit, Apple Watch, Garmin, Oura, etc.)
- **Differentiator:** Deepest HRV analytics on the market. AI personalisation engine. Wide wearable ecosystem support. 65% of subscribers report measurable improvements
- **Weaknesses:** Not BP-specific (general wellness), $12/mo pricing deters casual users, Trustpilot reviews significantly lower than App Store (support issues?)
- **Sources:** [Website](https://welltory.com/) | [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/welltory-heart-rate-monitor/id1074367771) | [Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/welltory) | [Braavo funding](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/welltory-secures-2-million-credit-line-from-braavo-capital-to-accelerate-ai-health-companion-launch-and-global-expansion-302604622.html) | [Tracxn](https://tracxn.com/d/companies/welltory/__9KPN61XW341bzOqZAPpiO2PsP0QZYGOPqtHesMOhxd0)

### 5. Heartify (Heartify Health)
- **Developer:** Heartify Health
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android
- **Pricing:** 3-day free trial, then subscription (~$80/year based on user reports). Weekly billing option available
- **App Store Rating:** 4.73/5 (~120K ratings on iOS)
- **Key features:** Heart rate + HRV measurement via phone camera, BP logging (manual input only), cardiovascular risk assessment quizzes, 10-year CV risk calculator, heart recovery rate tests, guided self-care challenges, mood tracking
- **Differentiator:** Emotional health angle — connects mood/lifestyle to heart health. Gamified challenges for behaviour change. Large rating count suggests strong user engagement
- **Weaknesses:** Misleading ads — promotes "blood pressure measurement" but only measures HR via camera (BP is manual logging). ~$80/yr is steep. No breathing exercises for BP reduction
- **Caution:** User complaints about bait-and-switch advertising (camera BP measurement implied but not delivered)
- **Sources:** [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/heartify-heart-health-monitor/id1546156891) | [Website](https://www.heartify.io/) | [Reviews](https://justuseapp.com/en/app/1546156891/heartify/reviews)

### 6. Cardiogram — EFFECTIVELY DEAD
- **Developer:** Cardiogram Inc. (co-founded 2016). Had Johnson & Johnson research partnership
- **Status:** Acquired by Ilumivu (Oct 2021). Ilumivu declared bankruptcy (2024). Assets sold to third party (2025). Last team members laid off late 2025. App is DOWN as of Nov 2025
- **Funding:** $2M total (Seed round, Oct 2016, 10 investors)
- **Pricing (was):** Free
- **Key features (were):** Continuous HR monitoring via Apple Watch/Wear OS, DeepHeart AI algorithm for predicting hypertension/sleep apnea/diabetes risk, personalised risk scorecards, sleep tracking
- **Differentiator (was):** Clinically validated AI on continuous wearable data. Research partnerships (J&J, UCSF)
- **Lesson:** Even well-funded, research-backed health apps die when parent companies fail. AI-first approach was ahead of its time but couldn't sustain as a business
- **Sources:** [Empirical Health alternatives](https://www.empirical.health/blog/cardiogram-heart-rate-alternatives/) | [Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/cardiogram) | [Ilumivu acquisition](https://www.nsmedicaldevices.com/deals/ilumivu-cardiogram-personalised-decision-support/)

## Adjacent Competitors (Breathing/Meditation)

### Apple Breathe / Mindfulness (Built-in watchOS)
- **What it is:** Renamed from "Breathe" to "Mindfulness" in watchOS 8. Built into every Apple Watch
- **Features:** Guided breathing animation (inhale/exhale with haptics), adjustable session duration (1-5 min), adjustable breath rate (4-10 breaths/min), Reflect sessions (mindful prompts), State of Mind emotional logging, mindful minutes tracked in Health app
- **Limitations:** Max 5-minute sessions. Apple Watch only (no standalone iPhone app). No BP tracking. Only one breathing pattern (no 4-7-8, box breathing, IMST etc.). Notifications can interrupt sessions. No variety of techniques. No progress tracking beyond minutes
- **Threat level:** LOW for BP market — too basic. But it raises public awareness of breathing for health, which benefits all competitors
- **Sources:** [Apple Support](https://support.apple.com/guide/watch/start-a-reflect-or-breathe-session-apd371dfe3d7/watchos) | [Pocket-lint guide](https://www.pocket-lint.com/mindfulness-on-apple-watch-what-does-it-offer-and-how-do-you-use-it/)

### Calm
- **Developer:** Calm.com Inc. (San Francisco)
- **Valuation:** $2B (Series C, 2020, led by Lightspeed Venture Partners)
- **Total funding:** $218M+
- **Revenue:** $596.4M (2024), up from $355M (2022). ~$7.7M/month in-app revenue (Jan 2024, highest-grossing health app worldwide)

- **Users:** 100M+ downloads
- **Key features:** Guided meditations, sleep stories (celebrity narrated), breathing exercises, mindful movement, music, Daily Calm. Calm Health (2022) — first clinical mental health product

## Market Gaps & Opportunities

### 1. Two major competitors just DIED (Qardio + Cardiogram)
- Qardio bankrupt, app removed from stores (late 2025)
- Cardiogram effectively dead after acquirer bankruptcy (2024-2025)
- Their displaced users are actively looking for alternatives NOW
- Community projects (LibreArm, OfflineQArm) show desperate user base

### 2. No one combines tracking + intervention + education well
- BreathNow is closest but is one-person dev team with UX issues
- SmartBP tracks but doesn't intervene
- Calm/Headspace intervene but don't track BP
- Heartify/InPulse track HR but BP logging is afterthought

### 3. Web-first gap
- ALL competitors are native apps (App Store / Google Play)
- A mobile-first PWA could reach users without App Store friction
- No install, no 30% Apple tax on subscriptions, instant access

### 4. Free-first monetisation
- Most competitors gate features early or use aggressive subscription tactics
- InPulse and Heartify have significant user complaints about billing
- A generous free tier builds trust in a health-sensitive market

### 5. Evidence-based messaging gap
- Most apps make vague wellness claims
- Leading with clinical citations (Craighead 2021 IMST study, etc.) builds credibility
- 2025 research from Carnegie Mellon confirms 10-21 min, 3x/week meditation apps deliver measurable BP results

### 6. IMST is massively underserved
- Only BreathNow includes it
- Craighead 2021 study is compelling: 5 min/day IMST lowered systolic BP by 9 mmHg
- Most apps don't even mention it

### 7. Apple Watch BP (rumoured for 2026-2027)
- When Apple adds BP measurement, the ecosystem will explode
- Being positioned early with Apple Watch integration is valuable
- Apps that already integrate with Apple Health will benefit most

### 8. UK/NHS angle
- NHS doesn't have a recommended BP app
- Getting NICE/NHS endorsement would be a massive distribution unlock
- UK hypertension prevalence: ~1 in 3 adults

### 9. Doctor report generation
- Few apps make it easy to generate a clean, clinical-grade report
- SmartBP does this well — but it's the ONLY differentiator they have
- Opportunity to combine tracking + intervention + professional reporting

### 10. Accessibility/underserved populations
- Rural areas lack access to in-person health coaching
- Emerging markets (Asia-Pacific 22% of breathing app market, growing fast)
- Non-English speakers — most competitors are English-only

### 11. Sustainability/trust concerns
- Qardio's collapse shows users don't trust VC-funded health startups
- Open-source or transparent data practices could differentiate
- Data portability (easy export, no lock-in) builds trust

## Competitive Positioning for BP Helper

**Angle:** Evidence-first, free, web-based BP tracker + breathing coach

| Our advantage | vs. Competition |
|---|---|
| PWA (no install) | Everyone else requires App Store download |
| Free-first | Most competitors gate aggressively |
| Clinical citations | Vague wellness claims elsewhere |
| Track + intervene + educate | No one does all three well |
| IMST included | Only BreathNow has it |
| Device-agnostic | Qardio died from hardware lock-in |
| Clean doctor reports | Only SmartBP competes here |

**Target:** Health-conscious adults 40-70 with diagnosed or pre-hypertension who want to actively reduce BP through evidence-based breathing, not just log numbers.
